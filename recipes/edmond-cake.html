Edmond Cake

A Delicous Experiment

I came up with this recipe when I was cleaning out the pantry. I had a bunch of tea biscuits (wafer cookies) and I didn't know what to do with them. After spending hours going down the Pinterest rabbit-hole, I found recipes that used wafer cookies layered with pastry cream. After some exprimentation, I made a version and took it to a pot-luck. My cousin's son said it was the best thing he ever tasted and asked for the name so his mother could make it for him. I told him that I didn't have a name for it, but since he liked it so much, I would name it after him. Edmond cake is born.

Ingredients

  • Approximately 45 tea biscuits
  • 1 batch of Easy Pastry Cream
  • 1 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 - 2 ounces shaved dark chocolate
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (your choice of almonds, pecans, walnuts, or pistachios), toasted if desired.

Directions

  1. Whisk the pastry cream so it is easy to spread and mix in the coconut.

  2. Place a layer of tea biscuits in the bottom of a 9 x 13 glass baking dish. Spread 1/2 of the pastry cream mixture evenly over the tea biscuits. Add another layer of tea biscuits and spread the rest of the pastry cream mixture over the biscuits and top with a final layer of tea biscuits.

  3. Whip the cream, sugar and vanilla until heavy peaks are formed. Spread over the top layer of biscuits. Sprinkle the top with the chocolate shavings and chopped nuts.

  4. Cover with plastic wrap and chill for 6 to 8 hours. Can be made a day ahead.
